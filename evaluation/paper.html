The population-view harness, as a standalone referee

popdgp formalizes the population-view harness — energy distance, calibration-blind coverage, classifier two-sample AUC, and an uncapped tail block, each anchored by a sampling-noise floor — as an eval-only package any candidate synthetic population can be scored against. The harness itself is real, proven, and shipping inside imputation-paper; the standalone popdgp methods paper formalizing it has not been started.

popdgp repository Read the harness in imputation-paper Strategy page
where the harness actually lives today
The working implementation, all four scoring blocks, and every published number are in imputation-paper (src/imputation_paper/experiments/). popdgp's own main branch is README-stage; a harness-extraction branch is in progress and unmerged.
what's proven
Floors behave correctly (energy 0.009–0.012, AUC ~0.50 on a survey's own holdout split); the block decomposition localizes failures a single metric misses (a weight-blind fit ties on energy distance and coverage while its tail runs 1.9–2.3× the holdout's).
what's not started
The standalone popdgp methods paper — formalization, a metric-blind-spot power study, and multi-view generalization — has no draft. Tracked as an open issue in the popdgp repository.
