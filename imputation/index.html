strategies / imputation

strategy 01 · imputation

Weight-native fill.

Every population estimate that combines two surveys needs to fill in variables one observes and the other doesn't. Fitting that fill model to survey records while ignoring their design weights biases the result toward the sample, not the population — and plants extreme low-weight donor values that a later reweighting can amplify into landmines.

01 — the 30-second version

Weighting is the whole effect.

populace's imputation operator is a regime-gated, sequentially-chained, weighted-bootstrap quantile-regression-forest estimator, built so an unweighted fit cannot be requested by accident. Under the harness below, weighting dominates every other design choice measured:

population-view tail inflation 1.9–2.3×the holdout's p99 geometry metrics miss it; the tail block catches it

From the imputation-paper abstract (draft; regenerates from committed run configs). The SCF's list-sample design oversamples wealthy households at low weight; an unweighted fit learns the sample's wealth distribution rather than the population's, and no other design choice in the estimator comes close to that effect size.

02 — the method

Three design choices, each measured separately.

weightingload-bearing

Weighted-bootstrap QRF

Draws training bootstraps proportional to design weight, so the fitted conditional distribution targets the population, not the sample. Cannot be disabled without an explicit weights="none" override — the interface makes the weight-blind failure mode a deliberate choice, not a silent default.

regime gatingshipped

Zero-inflated components

A gate model gets the zero/nonzero regime right before the forest fits the nonzero conditional. Holds dividend zero-share error to 0.6 points against 4.4 for the same forest ungated, and 25–84 points against linear and Gaussian baselines.

chainingshipped

Sequential, not independent

Multi-component targets (a four-part balance sheet) are drawn in sequence, each conditioned on the ones already drawn. Fitting them independently costs 5.8 points of two-sample AUC — a joint property invisible at two targets, binding at four.

benchmarked against unweighted & weighted QRF, OLS, linear quantile regression, and hot-deck matching

03 — how it's scored

The population-view harness.

Marginal fit metrics have a blind spot: an unconditional weighted draw that ignores every predictor can post the best pinball loss, because with predictors as weak as demographics and income are for wealth, marginal metrics alone cannot tell a method that draws from the correct weighted marginal from one that also gets the conditionals right. That observation motivates scoring the joint, not just the margin — the population-view harness, detailed on the evaluation strategy page: one latent population observed through survey views, each candidate file projected through a view and scored against that survey's own holdout on four blocks (energy distance, coverage, classifier AUC, an uncapped tail block), anchored by sampling-noise floors.

Hot-deck matching is a genuine near-peer under this harness — its classifier AUC (0.768) sits almost on top of the candidate's (0.761), both close to the indistinguishable floor. The classifier's real power shows up elsewhere: a weighted marginal draw that discards every predictor ties the candidate on energy distance yet gets caught at AUC 0.968 — near-perfectly separable — which is exactly the gap marginal metrics alone cannot see.

Installable on its own.
